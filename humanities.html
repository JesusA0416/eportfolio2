Humanities (HU) – Honors 2100

Intellectual Traditions: The Experience of Being

Human Experience Through Storytelling — aka the class that made me realize oral history is just ancestral chisme with citations. This page reflects my final project for Honors 2100, a course that asked what it means to be human by examining the stories we tell, how we tell them, and how they shape our identity, culture, and survival.

Signature Project: Becoming Selena

Selena wasn’t just a singer — she was the blueprint. A baddie before the word had hashtags. This project, “Becoming Selena”, reimagines her legacy as an anatomy chart because every part of her — the brain, the voice, the heart, the hands — tells a story of cultural defiance, dual identity, and beauty that broke barriers. She didn’t just perform; she stitched herself into culture. Through this visual piece, I turned her silhouette into a storytelling map of survival, joy, and generational impact. Period.

Reflection

This class made me rethink what a story is — and who gets to tell it. I came in thinking we’d just analyze literature, but we ended up analyzing ourselves. We questioned who’s visible in history, who gets erased, and how cultural identity isn’t something you inherit — it’s something you carry, reframe, and defend. Selena became the perfect canvas for that. Through her, I got to tell a story that wasn’t just academic — it was personal, historical, and emotional all at once.

This project let me explore the body as metaphor, as archive, as power. Her heart was Spanglish. Her eyes saw past borders. Her voice reached across generations. That anatomy chart? That was the only way to do her justice. But through that process, I started realizing that every part of me has also been shaped by language, movement, silence, and code-switching. I wasn’t just making a final. I was documenting how I’ve lived between two cultures and how I’ve started claiming space in both.

This class changed how I think, write, and express. It gave me space to reflect, not just react. And it reminded me that storytelling isn’t about performance — it’s about presence. I walked out of this course knowing that my voice matters, my perspective is valid, and even when I feel like I don’t belong, my story does. It belongs here.
